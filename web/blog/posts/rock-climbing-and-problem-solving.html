rock climbing and problem solving

18 June 2025

i've been climbing for a few years now, started it mostly because i needed something physical that wasn't a gym, the gym bores me after a while, climbing doesn't

what i didn't expect is how much it would change how i approach problems in general, a route on a wall is just a puzzle with physical consequences, you look at it, you try something, it doesn't work, you try something else, eventually you find the sequence that clicks

i've noticed i'm less frustrated when code breaks than i used to be, i think climbing trained something in me that's useful there, the belief that there is a way and you just haven't found it yet, that feels different from thinking something is broken and you just don't know why

the ego thing is the hardest part in both, you have to be willing to look bad while you're learning, to fall off the same move fifteen times in front of people, to ask someone for help on something that looks easy, once you get comfortable with that the actual learning speeds up a lot

i think more people who work in tech should do something physical that requires problem solving, not for the fitness, just for what it does to your relationship with failure
