my first trip to japan

14 February 2025

i flew to japan alone last week, hadn't planned it that far in advance, just bought the ticket when i had a few days off and needed to be somewhere that wasn't my apartment or my office

the first thing that hit me was the quiet, not the absence of noise exactly, just the absence of social noise, nobody performing, nobody trying to get your attention, everything works and nobody makes a big deal out of it

i think the reason japan is the perfect place to go when you're burned out is that the environment does all the work for you, you don't have to make many decisions, the trains tell you where to go, the konbini has everything you need at 2am, the food is good everywhere not just in the expensive places

i spent a lot of time just walking, which i hadn't done in months back home, walking without a destination and without my phone in my hand, it sounds like nothing but i think i needed it more than i realized

there's something about being in a place where you don't speak the language that is deeply restful, you can't eavesdrop on conversations, you can't get pulled into other people's urgency, you're just there inside your own head in a way that's hard to access at home

i came back wanting to fix things instead of just endure them, i don't fully know how to explain that but it's true, if you're burned out and you have any way to go there just go
