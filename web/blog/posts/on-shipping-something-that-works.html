on shipping something that works

20 September 2024

this website is real, you're on it, that still feels slightly unbelievable to me

i've had the idea for a personal site for years, started building one in 2021 with react and abandoned it when i got lost setting up the perfect component structure before writing a single line of actual content, classic, tried again in 2022 and stopped when the tooling got complicated and stopped feeling fun

this time i just used html and css, no framework, no build step, no dependencies, i finished it in a weekend

there's something important in that, the tool that lets you actually ship is usually the simpler one, not always, but more often than people want to admit, the time i spent learning react for a personal site that didn't need react wasn't entirely wasted but it wasn't the right tool for the thing i was actually trying to do

it's not impressive, it's small and a little rough, but it works and it's mine and that matters more than i expected it to, there's a difference between having built something and having almost built something and i didn't fully understand that difference until now
