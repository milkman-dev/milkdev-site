learning AI before you get left behind

20 January 2026

i've been thinking about how to say this without sounding like every linkedin post from 2023, bear with me

the hype around AI has made it feel like something you either caught or missed, like a train that already left, but the technology has only been genuinely accessible and useful for everyday work for about two years, the models that actually do something useful are younger than most of the side projects i've abandoned

that means the window to build real foundations is still open, the people who understand how these models work, what they're good at and what they're not, how to work with them well and when not to, those people are going to be more useful than the ones who just use the tools without understanding them

i'm not talking about becoming an AI researcher, i'm talking about understanding the basics, what a transformer does conceptually, what embeddings are, why context windows matter, how prompting actually works under the hood, this stuff is learnable in weeks not years and the resources are everywhere

the urgency is real though, not because AI will replace you directly but because someone who understands AI and uses it well will, and that distinction matters a lot, it's the same thing that happened with the internet and then with mobile and now with this

start now, it's genuinely not too late, but it also won't be true forever and the window is smaller than it was six months ago
