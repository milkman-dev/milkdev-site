slow progress is still progress

02 April 2025

i've been coding consistently for about three months now without breaking the streak, there were mornings where i sat down and didn't do much of anything useful, but i sat down and that's the part that matters

i used to stop when it stopped being fun or when i didn't have a clear idea of what to build next, now i just open the editor anyway and usually something comes, not always something good but something

the project i'm working on now is a small backend service that does one thing, i've restarted it twice because i kept overcomplicating it, the third version is genuinely simpler and that feels like a different kind of progress, knowing when to simplify is a skill i didn't know i was missing before

i think the shift happened somewhere around week six of the daily habit, the days stopped feeling like individual attempts and started feeling like part of something continuous, the bad days stopped feeling like failures and started feeling like just part of the process

slow is fine, slow is what's actually happening most of the time for most people, the fast versions you see online are almost always edited
